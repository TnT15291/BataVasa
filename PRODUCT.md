# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Productivity-focused individuals, people with ADHD, busy professionals, and self-improvement seekers. They use the app daily — often in short bursts, on the go, or in reflective moments. Primary tasks: logging a transaction quickly, checking habit progress, reviewing AI insights. They want to understand themselves, not just track numbers.

## Product Purpose

BataVasa is an AI-powered personal operating system. It unifies Finance, Habits, Journals, and Reminders into one surface and uses AI to surface behavioral patterns and meaningful insights across modules. Success = the user spends less time managing data and more time understanding themselves. The AI assists; it never overwhelms.

## Brand Personality

Calm · Intelligent · Personal

Voice: direct, warm, never clinical. Tone stays grounded even when surfacing difficult financial patterns. The product feels like a thoughtful collaborator, not a dashboard.

## Anti-references

- **Notion / generic productivity tools**: Too document-heavy, visually cold, lacks personality and warmth. BataVasa must feel like a living app, not a blank canvas.
- **Generic AI chat UIs**: Full-screen bubble chat, no structured data views, no sense of place. BataVasa has rich data structure — chat is one modality, not the identity.
- **Mint / YNAB-style finance apps**: Corporate green, heavy table grids, cold metrics-first layout. BataVasa's Finance module should feel personal and behavioral, not accounting-software formal.

## Design Principles

1. **Calm by default**: No loud color, no aggressive animations, no information overload. Density is earned by the task, not applied by default.
2. **Low friction, high insight**: Every interaction should be as fast as possible. The value is in what the app reveals, not in the entry mechanics.
3. **AI assists, never overwhelms**: AI output is surfaced inline, not as a modal interruption. Insights appear when relevant, collapse when not.
4. **Personal over generic**: Data views, labels, and patterns should feel tailored to the user's own data — not generic placeholders.
5. **Coherent across modules**: Finance, Habits, Journal, Reminders share one visual vocabulary. Switching modules should feel like moving rooms in the same home, not launching a different app.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best effort: no formal WCAG target, but follow reasonable contrast ratios (aim for AA on body text), support Dynamic Type, and maintain 44pt minimum touch targets. Support screen readers where feasible. Reduced motion respected via system preference.
